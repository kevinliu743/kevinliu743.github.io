The Mamba. The Legend.

I never imagined a day in which I would not be able to watch Kobe Bryant of the Los Angeles Lakers play again. But finally, on my 18th birthday, Kobe Bryant played his final game, of his legendary career.

Here's a time line of Kobe Bryant's life:

  • 08-1978 - Born in Philadelphia, while his father played for the 76ers
  • 07-1996 - Lakers trade for the 13th pick from the Charlotte Hornets in the 1996 NBA draft, and select 17 year-old Kobe Bryant
  • 1996-1997 - Named NBA All-Rookie second team, averaging 7.6 points per game off the bench
  • 1999-2000 - Wins first NBA Championship alongside Hall of Fame teammate Shaquille O'Neal, and coach Phil Jackson
  • 2000-2002 - Lakers are three-peat champions, and Kobe wins the 2002 All-Star game MVP
  • 07-2003 - Bryant is arrested and accused of sexual assault in Colorado
  • 2003-2004 - O'Neal and Bryant are in disagreement. O'Neal is traded, and Kobe almost signs with the Clippers, but ultimately decides to stay
  • 2005-2006 - Bryant records the second-highest scoring total in a game in NBA history as he torches the Toronto Raptors for 81 points
  • 2007-2008 - Lakers make a mid-season trade for Pau Gasol and the Lakers are a title contender once again. Kobe wins his single MVP award, but the Lakers lose to Boston in the NBA finals
  • 2008-2010 - Kobe wins his 4th and 5th rings. He is just 1 ring shy of the G.O.A.T.(Michael Jordan)'s 6 NBA titles
  • 04-2013 - Bryant suffers a torn Achilles tendon injury against the Golden State Warriors. He had been playing heavy minutes trying to get his Lakers into the playoffs. They made the playoffs, but without him due to his injury, the Lakers were swept in the first round
  • 04-2016 - On April 13, Kobe played his final NBA game. He scored 60 points on the night of his farewell game, and led the Lakers to a 101-96 victory. This was arguably the greatest farewell performance of all time, in any sport

A List of Some of Kobe's Greatest Achievements

  • 5-time NBA Champion
  • 2007-2008 regular season MVP
  • 18 All-Star selections
  • 15 All-NBA selections
  • 4 All-Star MVPs
  • 2 time NBA scoring champion
  • NBA Slam dunk champion
  • 33,643 career points- 3rd All-time
  • 2 Finals MVPs
  • 20 years with the Los Angeles Lakers
